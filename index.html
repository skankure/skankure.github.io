About Me

Hello, my name is Supriya and I am currently a student at Umass Amherst pursuing a degree in computer science. When I was in high school, I had the opportunity to take a course outside the standard curriculum: java programming. Initially, I was unsure of what to expect, but once I began the course, I fell in love with programming. I was fascinated by the power of just a few lines of code, as the possibilities were endless. I wanted to continue to learn about coding, so I subsequently took AP Computer Science and webpage design. I knew by my senior year of high school, that I wanted to major in computer science.

At college, I am pursuing a specialization in software engineering within my major. I am expected to graduate in May 2017. A highlight of my course work includes data structures, discrete mathematics, web programming, algorithms, software engineering, human computer interaction, product management, and information systems.

Outside of the classroom, I enjoy being able to have an impact on the community. I currently serve as an undergraduate co-chair for Umass Amherst's Computer Science Women's Group. Additionally, I have worked with CAITE, which is an organization that provides outreach for K-12 teachers to incorporate computer science in their curriculums. Currently, I am serving as a student programmer for a mobile app development project that will help underprivileged high school students stay up to par in biology courses.

I enjoy being a part of opportunities that challenge me and push me to be the best I can be. I enjoy programming, and aspire to be a software engineer in the future. I hope you enjoy my website. Thanks for visiting! Be sure to connect with me below.
